WHAT IS HEIGHT?

by Gonca Cara 28th September 2021

The Higher Education Growth for Sustainability Project, or HEight, is an initiative of a pan-European consortium of four forward-thinking higher education institutions (HEIs) and the  National Centre for Entrepreneurship in Education (NCEE), UK, that will draw of the innovate resources to spur on growth in the sustainable innovation in our communities and in our institutions. 

The partners Cyprus, Malta, Turkey and the UK will collaborate to deliver on our shared vision of prosperous, inclusive and climate-resilient societies where food systems and other areas of human activity that are more sustainable, trusted and healthy contribute to net zero carbon emission economies through a structured programme of activities based on a straight-forward, two-pillared concept: What? and How?

In the What we do pillar, we seek to develop excellence in teaching and learning of sustainable innovation and entrepreneurship, as well as to  collaborate with businesses and create new enterprises by specifically supporting start-up and spin-offs that contribute in the How we do it pillar.

We seek to foster the innovation potential within and across our institutions through deliberate cycles of action, reflection, learning and planning. These cycles are catalysed and informed  by the resources offered by HEInnovate and then both deepened and broadened by the insights and activities of consortium partners that aim to further integrate the three sides of the Knowledge Triangle - research, education and innovation.
